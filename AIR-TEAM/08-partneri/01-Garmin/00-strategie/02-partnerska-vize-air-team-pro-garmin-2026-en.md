# AIR TEAM Partnership Vision for Garmin 2026

## Purpose of the Document

This document serves as the main background material for a presentation intended for `Garmin` representatives. It is not a general introduction to `AIR TEAM`, but a clear expression of our partnership vision for 2026 and how we want to develop the `Garmin` brand more effectively, visibly, and systematically.

## Strategic Foundation

This document builds on the `AIR TEAM` strategy for 2025 and 2026, especially its emphasis on:

- completing the rebranding,
- more professional presentation of brands and products,
- trust, partnership, and customer centricity,
- stronger integration of sales, service, and marketing.

For the partnership with `Garmin`, this leads to a simple direction: we do not want to be only a distribution partner, but an active partner that strengthens the brand over the long term across the website, content, service, and the physical environment.

## Our Partnership Vision for Garmin

In 2026, we want to take the presentation of `Garmin` within `AIR TEAM` to a higher level. Our goal is for `Garmin` to be perceived not only as a strong product brand, but as a natural part of the expertise, service, and trust that customers associate with `AIR TEAM`.

This vision is built on four principles:

1. `Garmin` should be more visible.
2. `Garmin` should be better explained.
3. `Garmin` should be more strongly experienced in personal customer interactions.
4. `Garmin` should be supported over the long term, not only on a one-off basis.

## Main Priorities for 2026

### 1. Completion of the Rebranding and Better Presentation of Garmin Products

We want the `AIR TEAM` rebranding to be reflected in how we present partner brands as well. `Garmin` should be part of a more modern, clearer, and visually higher-quality presentation that matches both the level of the brand and customer expectations.

In practice, this means:

- more consistent visual presentation of `Garmin` across materials,
- higher-quality product and service materials,
- a more professional environment for both in-person and online interactions,
- clearer connection between the product, service, and expert consulting.

### 2. Stronger Web Presentation

We want to present `Garmin` on the website as a key brand in avionics and service solutions. The website should not serve only as a catalog, but as a trustworthy sales and information channel.

Main direction:

- create a clearer structure for `Garmin` products and solutions,
- better distinguish product communication from service communication,
- highlight our expertise, authorization, and service competence,
- make it easier for customers to understand what `Garmin` solves and why it is the right choice.

### 3. Educational and Content Support for the Brand

We do not want to communicate the `Garmin` brand only through product sheets. We want to build awareness through content that helps customers better understand the technologies, benefits, and practical applications.

The content should be based on these formats:

- blog articles,
- product news,
- explanatory educational content,
- case studies from installations and modernizations,
- short regular social media posts,
- newsletter blocks and themed mailings.

### 4. Long-Term Regular Communication

`Garmin` should be present in marketing communication on a continuous basis. We do not want to bring the brand forward only for a single event or campaign, but to consistently build its visibility and trust.

This means:

- planning content in a longer-term calendar,
- combining sales and educational communication,
- maintaining continuity across the website, blog, newsletter, and social media,
- regularly reinforcing the connection between `Garmin` and `AIR TEAM Service`.

### 5. Offline Visibility in the Kunovice Hangar

A strong partnership should not take place only online. We want customers to perceive the `Garmin` brand during personal visits, business meetings, and service interactions as well. That is why `Garmin` should become a visible part of the interior of the hangar in Kunovice.

This space should serve as tangible proof that we actively support the brand and can present it professionally in a physical environment as well.

### 6. Active Use of Garmin Cash Back

We want to use cash back in a purposeful and visible way. It should not be only a budget advantage, but a practical tool for brand growth within our environment.

We want to direct its use mainly into:

- marketing materials,
- promotional items,
- support for offline presentation,
- presentation elements in the hangar,
- visually high-quality outputs aligned with the new `AIR TEAM` branding.

## Marketing Plan 2026

### 1. Web and UX

### Objective

Improve the digital presentation of `Garmin` so the website can sell better, explain more clearly, and build trust more effectively.

### Key Steps

- create or adjust the website structure for clearer presentation of `Garmin` products,
- separate product information from service and consulting benefits,
- add stronger messaging around authorization, partnership, and service,
- improve customer navigation between inspiration, product information, and inquiry.

### Expected Effect

- better clarity,
- longer engagement with the content,
- more relevant inquiries,
- stronger perception of `AIR TEAM` as an expert partner for `Garmin`.

### 2. Content and Education

### Objective

Increase awareness of `Garmin` among customers through useful content that explains the value of the solutions and supports decision-making.

### Key Formats

- blogs,
- technical and educational articles,
- comparison content,
- case studies,
- short social media posts,
- content linked to news and seasonal topics.

### Content Themes

- why it makes sense to modernize a cockpit with `Garmin`,
- what benefits a new avionics solution brings,
- when service, upgrade, or replacement is the right choice,
- practical examples of use and real-world experience,
- connection between the product and `AIR TEAM` service support.

### 3. Regular Communication

### Objective

Ensure that the `Garmin` brand is continuously present in communication and does not lose continuity.

### Key Steps

- create a content calendar for the full year,
- plan regular posts according to themes and seasonality,
- include `Garmin` in newsletters and themed email campaigns,
- connect marketing outputs to sales and service priorities.

### Recommended Rhythm

- 1 to 2 themed blog posts per month,
- ongoing social posts throughout the month,
- regular newsletter inclusion,
- quarterly summaries of news, references, or service topics.

### 4. Offline Branding in Kunovice

### Objective

Turn the hangar in Kunovice into a space that naturally supports the perception of `Garmin` as a strong brand presented to a professional standard.

### Recommended Zones

#### Entrance and Customer Zone

- first visual contact with the brand,
- a branded wall or dominant graphic element,
- clear connection between `Garmin`, expertise, and service.

#### Presentation Zone

- a showcase or sample product display,
- presentation of selected solutions or applications,
- a space for business meetings and product presentations.

#### Service and Technical Zone

- subtle but visible navigation and signage,
- connection of the brand with authorized service,
- emphasis on professionalism rather than visual overload.

#### Meeting and Visitor Areas

- roll-ups, banners, and branded graphics,
- presentation materials and promotional items,
- consistent visual support during meetings.

### Application Principle

Offline elements must appear professional, clean, and trustworthy. The goal is not to overdecorate the hangar, but to create an environment that naturally confirms the quality of the partnership during each visit.

### 5. Brand Support and Use of Cash Back

### Objective

Use available `Garmin` cash back in a way that makes its impact directly visible in the quality of the brand presentation.

### Priorities for Use

- presentation and printed materials,
- promotional items for visits and business meetings,
- elements for the hangar in Kunovice,
- support for digital visuals and content outputs,
- ongoing renewal or addition of brand elements.
