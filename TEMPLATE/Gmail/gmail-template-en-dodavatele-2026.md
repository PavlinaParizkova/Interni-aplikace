## How to use this template

Each email follows the core AIR TEAM communication pattern:

> **Situation → Action → Result**

1. **Situation** – What is happening / what do we need to address?
2. **Action** – What are we asking for or proposing?
3. **Result** – What outcome are we working toward together?

Keep it short. One clear message per email. No filler. Speak like a person, not a system.

---

## Subject line examples

Use a subject that tells the recipient exactly what the email is about. No clickbait, no vague labels.

| Situation | Subject line |
|---|---|
| New enquiry / request for offer | `[AIR TEAM] Request for quote – [Product/Service]` |
| Order confirmation | `Order confirmed – [PO number / product name]` |
| Follow-up (no reply) | `Following up – [original topic]` |
| Thank you / wrap-up | `Thank you – [project/delivery name]` |
| Urgent request | `[Urgent] [Topic] – need your input by [date]` |
| General update | `Quick update – [topic]` |
| Scheduling a call | `Can we connect this week? – [topic]` |

## Greeting

Always use the person's first name. We have a warm working relationship with our suppliers.

```
Hi [Name],
```

For first contact or more formal situations:

```
Dear [Name],
```

Avoid: *"Dear Sir/Madam"*, *"To whom it may concern"*, *"Hello team"* (when writing to a specific person)

---

## Body structure

### Opening line – context / personal touch
Connect to something real. Reference the last conversation, a shared project, or a current situation.

```
[Situational opener – pick one or write your own]

- It was great talking at [event/call] last week.
- Hope things are going smoothly on your end.
- We're gearing up for [project/season] and I wanted to reach out.
- Quick one from our side –
- We're moving ahead with [project] and need your help with one thing.
```

Avoid: *"I hope this email finds you well."*, *"As per my previous email..."*, *"Please find attached..."*

---

### Core message – what you need

Be direct. One paragraph, one point.

```
[STATE THE SITUATION]
We're currently [context – ordering / planning / evaluating / finalising].

[STATE THE ACTION / REQUEST]
Could you [specific ask – confirm / send / check / advise]?

[STATE THE RESULT / GOAL]
This will help us [outcome – meet the deadline / finalise the order / move forward with the plan].
```

**Example:**
> We're finalising our Q2 order and need your updated pricing for [product]. Could you send us a revised quote by Friday? That'll let us confirm the PO next week and keep the timeline on track.

---

### Closing line – next step

Always end with a clear next step or an open door.

```
[Pick one or adapt]

- Let me know if you need anything from our side.
- Looking forward to hearing from you.
- Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier.
- Just let me know and I'll get things moving on our end.
- Thanks in advance – really appreciate your help on this.
```

Avoid: *"Please do not hesitate to contact me."*, *"Awaiting your earliest response."*, *"Kindly revert."*

## Full email examples

---

### 1. New enquiry / Request for offer

**Subject:** `[AIR TEAM] Request for quote – [Product/Service name]`

```
Hi [Name],

We're putting together our [Q2 / annual / project] plan and [product/service] is on the list.

Could you send us a quote for the following?

- [Item 1 – quantity, spec]
- [Item 2 – quantity, spec]

We're looking to confirm by [date], so if you can get back to us by [deadline], that would be perfect.

Let me know if you have any questions – happy to clarify.

Best,
[Name]
```

---

### 2. Order confirmation

**Subject:** `Order confirmed – [PO number / product name]`

```
Hi [Name],

Just confirming our order is ready to go on our end.

PO number: [XXXX]
Items: [brief description]
Requested delivery: [date]
Delivery address: [address or "as per our standard setup"]

Let me know once it's been processed. If anything looks off, just give me a shout.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

---

### 3. Follow-up (no reply)

**Subject:** `Following up – [original topic]`

```
Hi [Name],

Just looping back on my message from [date / "last week"] about [topic].

I know things get busy – just wanted to make sure it didn't slip through.

If you need more info from our side, I'm happy to help. Otherwise, even a quick heads-up on timing would be great.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

---

### 4. Thank you / wrap-up after delivery or project

**Subject:** `Thank you – [project/delivery name]`

```
Hi [Name],

The [delivery / project / event] went really well – thank you for your part in making it happen.

[Optional: one specific thing that worked well]

We'll definitely be coming back to you for [next project / next order]. Looking forward to it.

Best,
[Name]
```

---

### 5. Urgent request

**Subject:** `[Urgent] Stock check – [product name] – needed by [date]`

```
Hi [Name],

Sorry for the short notice – we have an urgent situation on our end.

We need [product/service] by [date] and wanted to check with you first before looking elsewhere.

Could you let me know ASAP if you can cover this? Even a rough confirmation would help us plan.

Really appreciate your help on this one.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

---

### 6. Scheduling a call

**Subject:** `Can we connect this week? – [topic]`

```
Hi [Name],

I'd love to catch up briefly about [topic / upcoming project / renewal / new product].

Are you free for a 20-minute call this week or early next? I'm flexible – just send me a few slots that work for you, or feel free to use [Calendly link / "my calendar link"].

Looking forward to it.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Tone of Voice – quick reference (EN)

### Use these
| Instead of... | Say this |
|---|---|
| "Please find attached" | "I've attached [X] below" / "Sending you [X] with this email" |
| "I hope this email finds you well" | Skip it, or: "Hope things are going well on your end." |
| "As per my last email" | "Just following up on [topic] from [date]" |
| "Please do not hesitate to contact me" | "Let me know if you have any questions" |
| "Kindly revert at your earliest convenience" | "Let me know when you get a chance" |
| "I am writing to enquire about..." | "Quick question about [topic] –" |
| "Please be informed that..." | Just state the information directly |
| "Going forward" | "From now on" / "For the next [project]" |

### Hero archetype in English
- Direct and clear – get to the point quickly
- Warm but not over-the-top – friendly, not sycophantic
- Concrete – say what you need and why it matters
- Respectful of their time – short emails, one clear ask
- Human – write like a colleague, not a contract

### Phrases that work
- "Quick one from our side –"
- "Just wanted to check in on [X]."
- "We're moving ahead with [X] and need your input."
- "Happy to jump on a call if that's easier."
- "Really appreciate your help on this."
- "Let me know if anything's unclear."

### Phrases to avoid
- "Leverage", "synergize", "circle back", "touch base", "moving the needle"
- "As discussed previously" (sounds passive-aggressive)
- "Per our conversation" (overly formal)
- "To whom it may concern" (impersonal)
- Any sentence starting with "Please be advised that..."

## Checklist before sending

- [ ] Subject line is specific and clear?
- [ ] Addressed to the right person by name?
- [ ] Opening line is natural – not a cliché?
- [ ] One clear message / ask in the email?
- [ ] Next step or expected action is obvious?
- [ ] Tone is warm and direct – not stiff, not over-familiar?
- [ ] No AI-style filler phrases?
- [ ] Signature is complete?
